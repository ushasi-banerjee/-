I have recently filed my dissertation in Linguistics at Yale University and expect to receive my Ph.D. in September 2026. I am interested in typological diversity and what it reveals about our capacity for language: what our basic communicative needs have in common across languages, and what any grammatical toolkit must be equipped with to meet them. My research primarily focuses on the confluence of linguistic form and meaning. A central aim of my research is to show that a comprehensive theory of language must take into account evidence from under-documented languages. To this end, the theoretical perspectives advanced in my dissertation emerged from my fieldwork on Khasi (Austroasiatic) and Mizo (Tibeto-Burman), which was supported by the MacMillan Center for International and Area Studies at Yale.
